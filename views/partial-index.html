Know the Risks

In the last 30 years, obesity rates have doubled in adults, tripled in children, and quadrupled in adolescents. -CSPIN


Each year, nearly 700,000 deaths in the U.S. are a result of poor diets alone. This figure accounts for nearly 20% of all U.S. deaths, and is the leading cause of death in the U.S. According to this livestrong article, reasons given for poor dietary habits are cited are as follows:

  1. Poor Dietary Foundations: Eating habits developed as a child, such as always cleaning your plate and eating microwaved convenience foods.
  2. Misinformation and Attitude: There are too many conflicting sources of information about nutrition. It can get confusing and complicated.
  3. Low Motivation: You know you're not eating healthy, but it doesn't cause any distress right now, so why bother?
  4. Slow Change: The results of a healthy lifestyle are slow and steady. Sometimes you don't see the results as quickly as you'd like, then give up.
